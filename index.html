Things to know before moving to New York City

Moving into a new environment is a big decision. It is a life-changing one too, you are torn between the neighborhood you should stay in, your funding, work opportunities, kids’ schooling, and others. If you don’t know anyone in the city you are moving into getting used to the city might be hard.

The following are the things you should know before moving to New York City.

Find the neighborhood that works for you

The neighborhood you pick will determine how you will enjoy your stay. So when picking this, be calm enough to pick the one that best suits you. There are lots of questions you should ask yourself when picking:

  • Is it near to your work or school place?

  • Is the neighborhood made for you?

  • Does it possess all the things you want in your desired neighborhood?

  • Is it peaceful?

  • Can you afford it?

These above-listed questions are some of the questions you should ask yourself. You won’t want to pick the wrong neighborhood, would you?

New York City has provided you with different neighborhood options you can pick from. NYC is made up of five boroughs, each of which has different flavors and vibes it offers.

The following are the boroughs that makeup New York City:

The Queens: Queens is filled with a variety of exquisite food hubs. It is a great neighborhood for lovers of nature, art fans, and sports fans. The vibe queens give off can not be gotten from other neighborhoods.

Manhattan: Manhattan is beautiful to the sight. It has numerous iconic neighborhoods, household-name attractions, and others. The neighborhood is filled with famous landmarks. Lastly, times square and Broadway can be found in their center.

Staten Island: This is named after the Staten Island Ferry. This ferry serves at its gateway. Staten Island is popularly known for its parking land, colonial villages, and importantly its beaches.

The Bronx: The Bronx has a little bit of hand-pulled mozzarella, home-run heroes, and hip-hop histories. It also has beautiful sight-worthy views like historic homes, charming parklands, and others.

Brooklyn: Brooklyn is like the heart of it all. It has everything to offer you. Its live music, sports, food, culture, and history makes it a great place to be. People from different parts of the world can be found in this neighborhood. The Brooklyn Bridge is one of the most magnificent sights to behold. It is everyone’s style.

It is pricey and competitive

New York is a busy town, to get through normal bills might be a little hard because of the high cost of living. Housing, basic utilities, and food all have high prices. The town has billionaires and average people likewise. Although, the average local might have to be in 2 to 3 jobs to make a real living.

Most people in New York are renting apartments. Having a roommate has now been the order of the day. This is because of the expensive housing allowance.

If you are coming from a small town, you will understand that apartments in New York is very small and pricey.

Searching for the apartment that works for you

In New York, getting the apartment you have your eyes on is not at easy as it sounds. There are lots of rental orders right before Labor day and Memorial day. If you are moving during that time, it will be more hectic than normal times. Although the heavy orders last for only some days.

The rental process

Before checking for apartments, It is advised to prepare your paperwork early. This way, you will be able to submit your application as soon as you see your dream apartment.

These applications include letters for referrals from your previous landlord, bank statement, payment slips, tax returns, employment letters, and many more.

Also, you need guarantors who can meet the minimum threshold. In case you have no guarantors, some companies will stand as your guarantor.

What You Need to Know About NYC Moving Costs and Options

Though, good properties that are not that pricy do come off the market quickly. Some brokers will charge you some fees to check out apartments. This whole process can cost you a fortune.

Some brokers do this service for free, i.e you don’t need to pay anything for this service. This way, you have saved some amount.

The prices of apartments vary depending on the amenities present in the apartment. The apartment that lacks elevators will charge a lower rent rate than modern apartments. Doorman, gym center, and other amenities can rise the price of the apartments.

Some companies list their available apartments on their site. This way you can easily pick what you can afford.
